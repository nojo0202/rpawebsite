SECTION 01

What is RPA Accounting?

RPA = Robotic Process Automation. RPA is technology that uses software to complete repetitive tasks done by humans.

The program follows steps given by a person (accountant) and then repeats those steps to complete clear, basic duties.

Essentially, RPA in accounting is like having a virtual assistant that does simple tasks for you — such as typing numbers from invoices into accounting systems, reading emails, matching transactions from bank to company records, and keeping up to date with Accounts Receivable and Payable.

SECTION 02

Impact on the Accounting Industry

Robotic Process Automation (RPA) is creating a major impact on the Accounting Industry. Studies have shown that RPA can significantly reduce costs and human error, while operating at much higher speeds and around the clock (Otero & Fink, 2020). For business managers this means lower labour costs, more accurate financial data, and more time for employees to focus on high value tasks rather than repetitive routine work.

SECTION 03

Advantages & Disadvantages

✓ Advantages

  • Uses the same interface and applications as users
  • Works effectively with several applications simultaneously
  • Approximately 80% faster than humans (Deloitte, 2025)
  • Works 24/7 → increasing efficiency
  • Allows employees to prioritize higher value tasks

✕ Disadvantages

  • High initial startup cost (ranging from $15,000 per bot to $20M+ for full systems for a large company (Solutions, 2023))
  • Needs to be updated whenever processes and systems change
  • Creates tension in the workplace and the industry as a whole due to fear of job loss
SECTION 04

RPA Software for Businesses

Small Businesses

Most companies like starting out using Microsoft Power Automate to automate menial accounting tasks on Microsoft applications like Excel.

Large Businesses

They prefer RPA applications like SAP Build and UiPath Automation Platform for flexible and customizable builds tailored to their accounting department.

Complex Builds

Companies prefer IBM RPA Studio for complex automation such as forecasting and analytics.

UiPath

UiPath is the most commonly used software among accounting firms.

Used by major companies including PWC, Deloitte, and Ernst & Young.

SECTION 05

Future of RPAs

$66B

Market Prediction

Experts value the RPA market at $3B in 2023, and predict it to grow anywhere between $6.7B to $66B by 2032 depending on growth trajectory.

Trajectory

Hyperautomation — the process where everything that can be automated is automated, fundamentally changing the role of an accountant.

What This Means

As RPA adoption accelerates, accountants will shift from manual data entry toward strategic analysis, oversight, and decision-making — roles that require uniquely human judgment.
